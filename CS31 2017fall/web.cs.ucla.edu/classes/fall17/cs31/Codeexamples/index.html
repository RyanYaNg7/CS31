Where the examples in this video use s.at(k) and t.at(k) to access characters in the strings s and t, use s[k] and t[k] instead — as the More Things about Strings writeup explains, if you have a bug in your program where you erroneously have k out of range, you may get more useful information at runtime if you use s[k] instead of s.at(k). This lecture is viewable in Firefox and Internet Explorer; it might not work in Chrome or Safari.
